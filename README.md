In many organizations, especially political parties, decision-making is centralized, and ordinarymembers have little say in the process. This makes these organizations vulnerable to corruption and canresult in decisions that do not reflect the will of the people. To address this issue, we have created theconcept of TrueRepublic (TR), a decentralized permanent democracy platform. TR makes the will of agroup transparent which enables grass-roots democracy. A key element of TR is its economic model,because it allows total decentralization, eliminates censorship and simultaneously offers protectionagainst troll attacks and manipulation. A incentive mechanism enables grass-roots decision-making andswarm intelligence in a secure and transparent manner, using modern evaluation methods like systemicconsensus. Transparency of will could enable "proxy-parties" which are political parties directlycontrolled by members. TR's incentive model and low technical barriers can facilitate participation,leading to grassroots decision-making and fighting corruption when it goes viral

The Pnyx (/nɪks, pəˈnɪks/; Ancient Greek: Πνύξ [pnýks]; Greek: Πνύκα, Pnyka) is a hill in central Athens where from 508 BC (reforms of Kleisthenes) to 330 BC the Athenians were hosting their popular assemblies, thus making the hill one of the earliest and most important sites in the creation of democracy.
